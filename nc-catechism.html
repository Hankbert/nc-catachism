Q1

What is our only hope in life and death?

That we are not our own but belong to God.

Romans 14:7-8

Q2

What is God?

God is the creator of everyone and everything.

Psalm 8-10 and 15

Q3

How many persons are there in God?

There are three persons in one God: the Father, the Son, and the Holy Spirit.

2 Corinthians 13:14

Q4

How and why did God create us?

God created us male and femaile in his own image to glorify him.

Genesis 1:27

Q5

What else did God create?

God created all things, and all his creation was very good.

Genesis 1:31

Q6

How can we glorify God?

By loving him and by obeying his commands and law.

Deuteronomy 11:1

Q7

What does the law of God require?

That we love God with all our heart, soul, mind, and strength; and love our neighbour as ourselves.

Matthew 22:37-40

Q8

What is the law of God stated in the Ten Commandments?

You shall have no other gods before me. You shall not make for yourself an idol. You shall not misuse the name of the LORD your God. Remember the Sabbath day by keeping it holy. Honour your father and your mother. You shall not murder. You shall not commit adultery. You shall not steal. You shall not give false testimony. You shall not covet.

Exodus 20:3and Deuteronomy 5:7

Q9

What does God require in the first, second, and third commandments?

First, that we know God as the only true God. Second, that we avoid all idolatry. Third, that we treat God's name with fear and reverence.

Deuteronomy 6:13-14

Q10

What does God require in the fourth and fifth commandments?

Fourth, that on the Sabbath day we spend time in worship of God. Fifth, that we love and honour our father and our mother.

Leviticus 19:3

Q11

What does God require in the sixth, seventh, and eighth commandments?

Sixth, that we do not hurt or hate our neighbour. Seventh, that we live purely and faithfully. Eighth, that we do not take without permission that which belongs to someone else.

Romans 13:9

Q12

What does God require in the ninth and tenth commandments?

Ninth, that we do not lie or deceive. Tenth, that we are content, not envying anyone.

James 2:8

Q13

Can anyone keep the law of God perfectly?

Since the fall, no human has been able to keep the law of God perfectly.

Romans 3:10-12

Q14

Did God create us unable to keep his law?

No, but because of the disobedience of Adam and Eve we are all born in sin and guilt, unable to keep God's law.

Romas 5:12

Q15

Since no one can keep the law, what is its purpose?

That we may know the holy nature of God, and the sinful nature of our hearts; and thus our need of a Saviour.

Romans 3:20

Q16

What is sin?

Sin is rejecting or ignoring God in the world he created, not being or doing what he requires in his law.

1 John 3:4

Q17

What is idolatry?

Idolatry is trusting in created things rather than the Creator.

Romans 1:21and 25

Q18

Will God allow our disobedience and idolatry to go unpunished?

No, God is righteously angry with our sins and will punish them both in this life, and in the life to come.

Ephesians 5:5-6

Q19

Is there any way to escape punishment and be brought back into God's favour?

Yes, God reconciles us to himself by a Redeemer.

Isaiah 53:10-11

Q20

Who is the Redeemer?

The only Redeemer is the Lord Jesus Christ.

1 Timothy 2:5

Q21

What sort of Redeemer is needed to bring us back to God?

One who is truly human and also truly God.

Isaiah 9:6

Q22

Why must the Redeemer be truly human?

That in human nature he might on our behalf perfectly obey the whole law and suffer the punishment for human sin.

Hebrews 2:17

Q23

Why must the Redeemer be truly God?

That because of his divine nature his obediences and suffering would be perfect and effective.

Acts 2:24

Q24

Why was is necessary for Christ, the Redeemer, to die?

Christ died willing in our place to deliver us from the power and penalty of sin and bring us back to God.

Colossians 1:21-22

Q25

Does Christ's death mean all our sins can be forgiven?

Yes, because Christ's death on the cross fully paid the penalty for our sin, God will remember our sins no more.

2 Corinthians 5:21

Q26

What else does Christ's death redeem?

Evey part of fallen creation.

Colossians 1:19-20

Q27

Are all people, just as they were lost through Adam, saved through Christ?

No, only those who are elected by God and united to Christ by faith.

Romans 5:17

Q28

What happens after death to those not united to Christ by faith?

They will be cast out from the presence of God, into hell, to be justly punished forever.

John 3:16-18 and 36

Q29

How can we be saved?

Only by faith in Jesus Christ and in his substitutionary atoning death on the cross.

Ephesians 2:8-9

Q30

What is faith in Jesus Christ?

Receiving and resting on him alone for salvation as he is offered to us in the gospel.

Galatians 2:20

Q31

What do we believe by true faith?

We believe in God the Father Almight, Maker of heaven andearth; and in Jesus Christ his only Son our Lord, who was conceived by the Holy Spirit, born of the virgin Mary, suffered under Pontius Pilate, was crucified, died, and was buried. He descended into hell. The third day he rose again from the dead. Heascended into heaven, and is seated at the right hand of God the Father Almighty; from there he will come to judge the living and the dead. We belive in the Holy Spirit, the communion of saints, the forgiveness of sins, the resurrection of the body, and the life everlasting.

Jude 1:3

Q32

What do justification and sanctification mean?

Justification means our declared righteousness before God. Santification means our gradual, growing righteousness.

1 Peter 1:1-2

Q33

Should those who have faith in Christ seek their salvation through their own works, or anywhere else?

No, everything necessary to salvation is found Christ.

Galatians 2:16

Q34

Since we are redeemed by grace alone, through Christ alone, must we still do good works and obey Gods word?

Yes, so that our lives may show love and gratitude to God; and so that by our godly behaviour others may be won to Christ.

1 Peter 2:9-12

Q35

Since we are redeemed by grace alone, through faith alone, where does this faith come from?

From the Holy Spirit.

Titus 3:4-6

Q36

What do we believe about the Holy Spirit?

That he is God, coeternal with the Father and the Son.

John 14:16-17

Q37

How does the Holy Spirit help us?

The Holy Spirit convicts us of our sin, and he enables us to pray and to understand God's Word.

Ephesians 6:17-18

Q38

What is prayer?

Prayer is pouring out our hearts to God.

Psalm 62:8

Q39

With what attitude should we pray?

With love, perseverance, and gratefulness.

Philippians 4:6

Q40

What should we pray?

The whole Word of God directs us in what we should pray.

Ephesians 3:14-21

Q41

What is the Lord's Prayer?

Our Father in heaven, hallowed be your name, your kingdom come, you will be done, on earth as it is in heaven. Give us today our daily bread. And forgive us our debts, as we also have forgiven our debtors. And lead us not into temptation, bue deliver us from evil.

Matthew 6:9

Q42

How is the Word of God to be read and heard?

With diligence, preparation, and prayer; so tha we may accept it with faith and practice it in our lives.

2 Timothy 3:16-17

Q43

What are the sacraments or ordinances?

Baptism and the Lord's Supper.

Romans 6:4and Luke 22:19-20

Q44

What is baptism?

Baptism is the wahsing with water in the name of the Father, the Son, and the Holy Spirit.

Matthew 28:19

Q45

Is baptism with water the washing away of sin itself?

No, only the blodd of Christ can cleanse us from sin.

Luke 3:16

Q46

What is the Lord's Supper?

Christ commanded all Christians to eat bread and to drink from the cup in thankful remembrance of him.

1 Corinthians 11:23-26

Q47

Does the Lord's Supper add anything to Christ's atoning work?

No, Christ died once for all.

1 Peter 3:18

Q48

What is the church?

A community elected for eternal life and united by faith, who love, follow, learn from, and worship God together.

2 Thessalonians 2:13

Q49

Where is Christ now?

Christ rose bodily from the grave on the third day after his death and is seated at the right hand of the Father.

Ephesians 1:21

Q50

What does Christ's resurrection mean for us?

Christ triumphed over sin and death so that all who trust in him are raised to new life in this word and to everlasting life in the world to come.

1 Thessalonians 4:13-14

Q51

Of what advantage to us is Christ's ascension?

Christ is now advocating for us in the presence of his Father and also sends us his Spirit.

Romans 8:34

Q52

What hope does everlasting life hold for us?

That we will live with and enjoy God forever in the new heaven and the new earth, where we will be forever fred from all sin in a renewed, restored creation.

Revelation 21:1-4
